COVID-19 & MENTAL HEALTH

Since the pandemic began in March 2020, the talk of mental health has become more widespread with many studies saying it has become even more prominent in our society. Many people struggle with anxiety, depression, addiction and other things that can consistute in the cateogory of mental health.

Here are some basic terms to learn and know when discussing mental health.

Over the past year mental health has declined. 1 in 5 persons who had COVID-19 are diagnosed with a mental illness. In the US 42% of CDC survey respondants reported symptoms of anxiety or depression. This is a 200% increase from 2019. Havard University research found that out of 224 children aged 7-15 66% had clinically significant signs of anxiety or depression. Mental health is declining in most ages group of the US and wordly population.
